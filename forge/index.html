01 · The shift · Industry 4.0 → 5.0

From fragmented ERP / MES / WMS to one OS.

Today's EMS challenges
  • · Fragmented systems · ERP, MES, WMS, HRMS, spreadsheets each siloed
  • · Disconnected traceability · SN, IMEI, MAC IDs tracked inconsistently across stages
  • · Manual quality checks · SPI, AOI, X-Ray done reactively · no closed-loop automation
  • · Inefficient procurement · vendor onboarding, PO approvals, GRN reconciliation manual
  • · No real-time visibility · WIP and FG statuses updated by hand · no SLA alerts
  • · Costly downtime & rework · failures found late · low FPY
With Forge
  • · Unified Manufacturing OS · one AI-native digital backbone linking BOM, work orders, SN traceability, HRMS, QMS
  • · End-to-end genealogy · unique SN/IMEI binding from PCB → Assembly → QA → Carton → Shipment
  • · AI-powered quality · in-line defect detection · FPY monitoring · automated loopbacks
  • · Streamlined Procure-to-Pay · automated POs, vendor scorecards, 3-way matching, predictive replenishment
  • · Live dashboards & control tower · real-time WIP, OEE, SLA alerts
  • · Predictive maintenance · 30–50% downtime reduction · +2pp FPY

PILOT · NEOLYNC TIRUPATI · PHASE 1 · MES Platform003 / 006
04 · Pilot · NeoLync Tirupati · Phase 1

Live at NeoLync Tirupati · Jio Air Fiber Wi-Fi.

Forge is deployed at NeoLync's Tirupati facility supporting Jio Air Fiber Wi-Fi device manufacturing. Current production run-rate ~1,000 units. Platform handles peaks of 3,000 TPS during high-volume runs. 50+ operators and 25 line managers are on Forge today. Deployment is 100% on-premise. High reliability, complete data control, low-latency operations. End-to-end traceability across the entire production process. Pilot rollout in progress; plan is to extend Forge across all production sites and product lines within the plant.

Go-live validation runs against a 15-category checklist (v2.0 · 07-Apr-2026). Phase 1 covers planning & traceability, quality & repair, scan-pack, ASN, and predictive maintenance. Each item carries a Fynd development status, a NeoLync site readiness status, a DRI / reviewer pair, and a target date.

15 categories. Five themes.

The go-live checklist (v2.0 · 07-Apr-2026) groups into five readiness themes. Each carries Fynd development status, NeoLync site readiness, a DRI/reviewer pair, and a target date.

Theme 01 · Masters

Product, material, BOM.

FG / SFG SKUs with attributes, images, and customer mapping. Raw material masters with UoM and storage classification. Multi-rev BOM with component substitution and ECO traceability.

Categories: 01 Product Master · 02 Material Master & Inventory · 03 Bill of Materials.
Theme 02 · Floor

Routing, stations, infra.

Site → Line → Station hierarchy with digital work instructions. Machine and equipment configuration with network connectivity. Forge edge install with machine log reading and reliable data sync.

Categories: 04 Routing & Stations · 05 Line Infra & Machine Readiness · 06 Edge Client & Automation.
Theme 03 · Traceability

Serialise, assemble, schedule.

SN / RSN / IMEI / MAC / PSN binding rules at unit level. Assembly readiness and packing configuration. Shift planning, work order release, skill-based allocation.

Categories: 07 Serialisation & Identifiers · 08 Assembly & Packing · 09 Shift & Scheduling.
Theme 04 · Quality & ship

Test, invoice, dispatch.

Pre-assembly PCBA testing and post-assembly FATP testing under one QMS. Customer order book, invoicing, documentation. ASN, e-invoice, and dispatch flow tied to ERP.

Categories: 10 Testing & QMS · 11 Order Management & Invoicing · 12 Shipping & ASN.
Theme 05 · Cockpit

Dashboards, access, go-live.

Live OEE, FPY, downtime, throughput, SLA dashboards. RBAC, role-based UI, operator training. Dry run, go-live cutover, hypercare window.

Categories: 13 Dashboards & KPIs · 14 RBAC & Training · 15 Go-Live, Dry Run & Hypercare.

ROADMAP · PILOT TO PLANT-WIDE · MES Platform005 / 006
06 · Roadmap · pilot to plant-wide

Four phases. One factory becoming the template.

Forge moves from a single pilot line at NeoLync Tirupati to plant-wide rollout, then to a multi-plant footprint, then to a fully agentic Industry 5.0 stack. Each phase carries its own readiness gate.

Phase 1 · Pilot
Live

NeoLync Tirupati · Jio Air Fiber.

Forge live for Jio Air Fiber Wi-Fi device manufacturing. ~1,000 units run-rate, 3,000 TPS peak, 50+ operators and 25 line managers on platform. 100% on-premise.

Exit: 15-category go-live checklist v2.0 cleared with Fynd dev + NeoLync site sign-off.
Phase 2 · Plant-wide
Building

All lines, all product families.

Forge extended across every production line and product family in the plant. Plan, Buy/Source, Assemble, Move on the same data spine. Hypercare wraps each new line.

Exit: all plant lines on Forge with steady OEE and FPY for four consecutive weeks.
Phase 3 · Multi-plant
Roadmap

Reference build → repeatable rollout.

Tirupati becomes the reference factory. Forge rolls out to additional manufacturing sites with the on-premise deployment kit, edge client, and the validated checklist as the cutover playbook.

Exit: second site live with the same 15-category gate cleared inside the agreed window.
Phase 4 · Industry 5.0
Roadmap

Agentic AI across the floor.

The four agentic loops (planning, inventory, quality, predictive maintenance) move from human-in-loop to self-optimising. AI Cockpit surfaces explainable recommendations to plant leadership.

Exit: agentic loops measured against target KPIs in §05 across two consecutive quarters.
