02 · What it does for you

Buyers and planners raise fabric requests through structured forms instead of ad-hoc emails and WhatsApp messages, with mandatory fields and audit trails. Format heads approve fabric budgets in a step-by-step workflow (planner submits → format head reviews → finance approves) with the option to approve fully, partially, or send back for revision. Mill suppliers receive orders based on intelligent recommendations considering their production capacity, capabilities and past performance and can accept or reject with real-time tracking.

03 · How Much Is Being Managed
₹420 crore+ in fabric orders on platform₹258 crore (~67%) assigned to suppliers₹127 crore in active buyer decisions₹34.6 crore in early-stage pipeline~3,650 metric tonnes of weft knit fabric~3.08 crore metres of woven/warp/denim fabric38 improvements shipped in March alone

Meaningful adoption across the full fabric ordering workflow, most value already in advanced stages (supplier assignment and buyer decisions).

04 · What improved

Buyer can no longer accidentally over-order fabric, the system enforces AOP (Annual Operating Plan) limits. Planners have clear visibility into how much OTB budget is available at each level. Format heads maintain control over procurement decisions with approve/reject/revise workflows. Mill suppliers get allocated work based on their actual track record, not just whether they have capacity. 38 improvements shipped in one month (March).

Expected metric impactover-ordering reduced (every rupee of fabric over-purchase ties up working capital), budget compliance improved, format-level accountability established. ₹420Cr+ now governed on-platform, compared to zero 6 months ago.
