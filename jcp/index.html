01 · The bet

78 stacks. Or one.

Reliance Retail runs 78 commerce channels across 10 verticals: fashion, grocery, electronics, beauty, furniture, pharma, jewellery, home decor, fresh food, digital top-up. Built channel-by-channel, that is 78 codebases, 78 OMS instances, 78 release trains, 78 catalog systems, 78 AI roadmaps. Every brand pays its own engineering tax. Every breakthrough has to be re-built 78 times.

JCP is the bet that one stack does it instead. One codebase. One release train. One AI substrate. Every brand inherits, no brand loses its identity.

The math only works if the platform holds at peak. It does. 350K orders / hour tested · 700K+ orders / day live. Vertex Search shipped to 5 channels in one cutover, not 5 projects. Web Grounding shipped to Netmeds, Reliance Digital and Tira off one pipeline.

Before · the tax

78 separate stacks.

  • · 78 codebases to keep alive
  • · 78 release trains to coordinate
  • · 78 catalogs to enrich
  • · Every AI capability built 78 times
  • · Brand teams blocked on shared ops
After · one stack

One stack. 78 brands.

  • · One codebase, one release train
  • · Each capability builds once · ships everywhere
  • · Vertex Search · 5 channels · 1 cutover
  • · Web Grounding · 3 brands · 1 pipeline
  • · 200M+ customers on the same substrate
What it took

Discipline at the core, freedom at the edge.

  • · Microservice tenant isolation
  • · Strict release trains across verticals
  • · Brand identity preserved at the edge
  • · Infrastructure compounded at the core
  • · AI built into the substrate, not bolted on

VI.AI SHIPPED · Enterprise Commerce006 / 010
07 · AI shipped

Search, cataloging, recommendations · live.

Three AI capabilities shipped on JCP this year (search, cataloging, recommendations) measured against live A/B traffic, not synthetic benchmarks. Next wave: agentic commerce on top of the same substrate.

Search · Google Vertex

+7.4–10.7% revenue per search.

Live across 5 channels: Tira, Netmeds, Reliance Digital, Swadesh, Urban Ladder. 90M+ production searches across 70+ days of A/B versus Algolia. Zero-result queries dropped 53–75%. Click-through up 23% on Tira. Cutover happened with zero downtime.

5 channels · 90M+ searches · zero-downtime migration

Cataloging · Web Grounding

From 40 hours to 2 minutes per 1,000 SKUs.

Netmeds: 95%+ enrichment time eliminated, vendor cost (~₹100/SKU) collapsed to near-zero. Reliance Digital: 35–40% faster, 75% first-pass auto-fill, 50–64% throughput gain on the same team. Tira × Akind × Google: 10–15 min/SKU → ~14 sec, 332 of 382 attributes filled, 89% accuracy vs ground truth.

IX.ROADMAP · Q2 AMJ 2026 · Enterprise Commerce009 / 010
10 · Roadmap · Q2 AMJ 2026

90 Day Roadmap.

Money flows · 55 features

Promotions, payments, cart.

The largest pillar of the cycle. 27 promotion features consolidate the unified offers stack: serial coupons, cashback flows, BIN targeting, EMI offers, period limits, free shipping, RBPLT thresholds. Payments V2 ships a new endpoint, multi-MoP support, UPI QR, BillDesk and ICICI as aggregators, plus a payment ranking engine. Cart migrates address flows to Atlas gRPC and adds the fraud engine integration.

27 promotions · 14 payments · 10 cart · 4 RBPLT · 3 user-groups

Operations spine · 54 features

Catalogue, serviceability, OMS.

The second-largest theme of the cycle. Catalogue 24 features: multi-category support, hierarchy templates v2, attribute overhaul, SKU-level collections, Discount V2 with 4-level cascade. Serviceability 23 features: zone maker-checker, multi-mode fulfilment (Express, Quick, Same-Day), dark-store classification, Clickpost for international, GWP cross-warehouse fulfilment. OMS 7 features centred on Herald promise integration and reconciliation.

24 catalogue · 23 serviceability · 7 OMS

Storefront · 23 features

CMS, navigation, search, size.

CMS sections get a full lifecycle with maker-checker and a multi-user lock system. Search adds AJIO RRA recommendations and a JCP product recommendation engine over Solr + MongoDB with Vertex AI provider selection. Size & Fit v1.0 ships 42 capabilities for AJIO and JCP; v2.0 introduces body-measurement input and cross-brand size translation. SEO ships a unified sitemap admin with 7 sub-modules and 22 business rules.

7 CMS · 5 navigation · 3 search · 5 size · 3 SEO

Foundation · 31 features

Console, infrastructure, files.

12 platform-wide infrastructure upgrades are the spine: fit.js v2.6 → v3.4 across 30+ services, Node.js 24 + pnpm, Mongoose 8 / MongoDB 8 compatibility, SSH → HTTPS ADO migration, Fortify / BlackDuck remediation, the AI-Native service template (CLAUDE.md, ADRs, event schemas) rolled out across all 49 services. Platform Console adds Nexus deployment aggregator, ad builder, application-migration UI, Microsoft Clarity + PostHog analytics.

12 infra · 8 console · 5 file storage · 4 webhooks · 2 data

Trust and identity · 12 features

Identity, OTP, compliance.

A small but security-critical theme. OTP cross-flow vulnerability closed (auth flows can no longer replay each other's tokens). Atlas Go migration on identity. Slingshot PII bug fix. JioCX wired in as the SMS provider. Pluggable GST + PAN verification provider config for seller onboarding.

OTP · identity · PII · SMS provider · GST + PAN verification
